Why Coco Peat

The growing medium that grows on you.

A renewable, sustainable replacement for peat moss with better water retention, aeration and root health than most traditional substrates.

What is it?

From coconut by-product to premium substrate.

Coco peat (also called coir pith) is the fine, spongy material extracted from the husk between the outer shell and the coconut fibre. Once dried, washed and compressed, it becomes one of the most versatile growing media on the market.

Unlike peat moss which is harvested from ancient peat bogs and cannot be replenished within human timescales coco peat is a fully renewable resource produced every year from the global coconut harvest.

Benefits

Why growers switch.

Holds 8–10x its weight in water

Dramatically reduces irrigation frequency compared to traditional soil.

Excellent aeration

Fibrous structure delivers oxygen to roots, accelerating plant growth.

Stable, near-neutral pH

Naturally falls in the 5.5–6.5 range, ideal for most crops.

100% renewable

A by-product of coconut processing no peat bogs harmed.

Applications

Where it's used.

Horticulture

Container growing for ornamentals, vegetables and herbs.

Greenhouse Production

Tomatoes, cucumbers, strawberries and soft fruit on grow bags.

Erosion Control

Geotextiles and biologs for slopes, shorelines and restoration.

Hydroponics

Excellent water-holding substrate for drip-fed systems.

Nursery Propagation

Coir discs and plugs for seed starting and cuttings.

Landscaping & Mulch

Decorative chips and mulch for water-wise gardens.
